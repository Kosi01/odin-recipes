Description

spaghetti is a thin, straight, cylindrical pasta of Italian origin, the most popular of all pastas eaten today. Noodles made of wheat and eggs were known in the Mediterranean world in antiquity, but the pasta we know as spaghetti—which takes its name from the Italian spaghetto, “little cord”—was probably introduced to Sicily by the island's Arab conquerors in the 8th century.
